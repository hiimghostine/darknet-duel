### What is the ShadowNet?

The ShadowNet is the hidden backbone of the digital world ‚Äî an underlayer of the internet created decades ago by intelligence agencies, defense contractors, and megacorporations. Originally designed as a failsafe network in case the public internet collapsed, it was built to be faster, decentralized, and resilient.

But over time, it leaked. Hackers, rogue AIs, and underground groups discovered ways into it. Now, it's a lawless arena where cyberwars are fought, invisible to the average user who still thinks the "internet" is all there is.

Think of it as the dark ocean beneath the internet's surface web ‚Äî whoever controls the ShadowNet can manipulate finance, communications, and power grids worldwide.

### The Digital Underworld

In the near future, the digital world has become more valuable than oil, more dangerous than warzones. Nations, corporations, and syndicates all battle in the ShadowNet, where the entire world's infrastructure is interconnected. Whoever controls it holds the keys to power, wealth, and chaos.

But in this world, firewalls are castles and malware is artillery. Cybersecurity isn't defense‚Äîit's war.

## üëë WHO OWNS THE SHADOWNET?

Here's the catch: **no one truly owns the ShadowNet** ‚Äî but many claim pieces of it. It's like the deep sea: uncharted, dangerous, full of treasures and monsters.

### The Claimants

**Governments:**
- Secret agencies use it to run covert ops and spy programs
- They control "choke nodes" ‚Äî points where data can be intercepted or killed

**Megacorporations:**
- Tech giants and banks built their own private subnetworks inside the ShadowNet
- They defend them fiercely, because their whole empire runs on it

**Syndicates & Collectives:**
- Groups like PHOENIX_NET carve out hostile regions
- Using ShadowNet as both battlefield and breeding ground for zero-days and botnets

**Defenders of Order:**
- Entities like GHOST_CORP try to keep ShadowNet stable
- Not because they own it, but because if ShadowNet collapses, so does the world above it

**The Architects (Myth):**
- Old legends say ShadowNet was actually built by a collective of rogue scientists and early AI experiments
- Somewhere in its depths lies the **Root Protocol** ‚Äî the master key to the entire network
- No one knows if it's real, but both sides search for it

## ‚öîÔ∏è THE WAR FOR THE INFRA

The Infrastructure represents the shared global backbone‚Äîcloud servers, financial systems, communications satellites, power grids. Both sides are locked in a constant cycle of attack and defense:

- **PHOENIX_NET** seeks to seize nodes, cripple defenses, and spread chaos until the system collapses under their control

Each card represents tools, exploits, countermeasures, tactics, and legendary operatives. The battlefield is not a map of land, but a network of servers, firewalls, databases, and control nodes.

### The Stakes

**To PHOENIX_NET:** ShadowNet is the perfect weapon. If they seize enough of it, they can burn down the old order and rebuild a new one.

**To GHOST_CORP:** ShadowNet is the fragile thread holding civilization together. If it falls, the surface world descends into chaos.

Thus, every battle (every match in Darknet Duel) is really a war for control over fragments of ShadowNet's critical infrastructure.
