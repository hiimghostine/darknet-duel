Use Case: Turn-Based Gameplay, AP Allocation, Card Play, Targeting, and Reaction Phase

Description: Allows players to take turns, receive action points (AP), play cards from their hand, select targets for card effects, and handle a reaction phase where the opponent may respond to a card play. The system enforces turn order, validates actions, manages the reaction phase, and updates the game state accordingly.

Actors: Player

Preconditions: Game session is active and player is authenticated and in the game.

Main Flow:
1. System determines which player's turn it is.
2. System allocates AP to the active player.
3. Player selects and plays a card from their hand.
4. Player selects targets for the card (if required).
5. System enters the reaction phase, temporarily handing control to the opponent.
6. Opponent may play a reaction/counter card or pass.
7. System validates the reaction (if any) and resolves card effects in the correct order.
8. System updates the game state and notifies all players.
9. Turn passes to the next player.

Postconditions: Game state is updated after each turn, including any reactions; all players are notified of actions and state changes.
